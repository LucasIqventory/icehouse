# Ice House Tailgating — style.md

## Design Philosophy

Ice House Tailgating should feel like it was carved from a glacier and engineered for game day.

This is not a winter website.

This is not a holiday website.

This is not a ski resort website.

The visual language should communicate:

* Cold
* Precision
* Power
* Engineering
* Durability
* Energy
* Atmosphere

The website should feel like a frozen machine built specifically for tailgating.

Every visual decision should reinforce the feeling of ice, steel, and performance.

---

# Brand Identity

Ice House exists at the intersection of:

* Arctic environments
* Stadium energy
* Tailgating culture
* Utility trailers
* Industrial design

The brand should feel rugged and premium.

Not luxury.

Not corporate.

Not playful.

Not futuristic.

Think:

A custom-built trailer parked on a frozen lake under stadium lights.

---

# Core Visual Rules

## Use

* Sharp edges
* Angular geometry
* Crystal-inspired shapes
* Ice textures
* Frost patterns
* Steel surfaces
* Blue illumination
* High contrast

## Avoid

* Rounded corners
* Soft blobs
* Generic gradients
* Glassmorphism
* Floating cards
* Snowman imagery
* Holiday aesthetics
* Cozy cabin aesthetics
* Ski resort aesthetics
* Corporate SaaS design

# Surface Design

Every surface should feel physical.

Not digital.

Examples:

* Frosted steel
* Cracked ice
* Glacier faces
* Frozen metal
* Ice-covered aluminum

Backgrounds should use subtle texture overlays.

Never flat color.

Never obvious stock textures.

---

# Geometry

The website should be built from shards.

Not boxes.

Preferred shapes:

* Trapezoids
* Angled panels
* Ice fragments
* Crystal formations
* Fracture patterns

Use diagonal cuts throughout layouts.

Avoid generic rectangular sections whenever possible.

---

# Borders

Borders should resemble:

* Ice fractures
* Frost lines
* Frozen seams

Examples:

Thin glowing blue edge

Frost accumulation in corners

Crystalline corner details

---

# Typography

## Headlines

Font Style:

Industrial
Condensed
Mechanical

Recommended:

* Rajdhani
* Eurostile
* DIN Condensed
* Agency FB

Headlines should feel like equipment markings.

Examples:

ICE HOUSE

OFF-GRID POWER

GAME DAY READY

BUILT FOR KICKOFF

---

## Body Text

Font:

Inter

Weight:

400–500

Simple and readable.

No decorative body typography.

---

# Hero Section

The hero should immediately communicate:

Cold. Power. Capability.

Background:

A massive wall of frozen ice with subtle internal lighting.

The ice should feel deep and heavy.

Not decorative.

Not cartoonish.

Hero image should feature the trailer illuminated against dark surroundings.

Stadium lights.

Blue reflections.

Sharp contrast.

---

# Motion Design

Animations should feel physical.

Not playful.

## Approved

Frost growth

Ice crack propagation

Light refracting through ice

Frozen shimmer

Crystal formation

---

## Forbidden

Bouncing elements

Floating cards

Parallax overload

Snowflake explosions

Confetti

Particle storms

---

# Product Presentation

Products should be treated like equipment.

Not lifestyle products.

Display specifications clearly.

Highlight:

* Battery systems
* Off-grid capability
* Entertainment systems
* Climate control
* Storage solutions

Use technical visual language.

Blueprint-inspired layouts.

Measurement callouts.

Engineering details.

---

# Photography Direction

Photography should emphasize:

* Texture
* Materials
* Lighting
* Utility

Show:

* Ice-covered surfaces
* Frozen condensation
* Blue reflections
* Trailer details
* Tailgating atmosphere

Avoid:

* Generic smiling families
* Stock photos
* Corporate handshakes
* Lifestyle clichés

---

# User Experience

The website should feel cold but not hostile.

Powerful but not aggressive.

Premium but not luxury.

Visitors should feel:

"I need one of these."

Not:

"This looks like a template."

---

# Design References

Use inspiration from:

* Arctic expedition equipment
* Icebreaker ships
* Industrial vehicle design
* NHL arena tunnels
* Stadium infrastructure
* Frozen lakes at night
* Crystalline ice formations

Do not use inspiration from:

* SaaS websites
* AI startup landing pages
* Crypto websites
* Holiday websites
* Ski resorts
* Generic outdoor brands

---

# Final Design Directive

Build the website as if every component was cut from a glacier using industrial machinery.

The interface should feel cold, sharp, engineered, and unforgettable.

Ice is not decoration.

Ice is the material the entire brand is made from.
